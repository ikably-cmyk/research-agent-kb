## Key Points

- Bleaching agents (16% and 30% hydrogen peroxide) caused a decrease in enamel microhardness and surface roughness compared to initial values.
- After a demineralization-remineralization (pH) cycle with sodium fluoride toothpaste, microhardness values increased in bleached groups, but the increase was not statistically significant (P< 0.05).
- Surface roughness values increased significantly after the pH cycle, with significant differences between the 4th measurement and other time points (P< 0.05).
- FE-SEM analysis at x1,000 and x5,000 magnifications was used to evaluate surface morphology changes.
- Remineralizing agents applied during the pH cycle may have a positive effect on enamel microhardness following bleaching.
- 32 extracted premolar teeth were used; samples were immersed in coffee solution for 1 week to simulate oral discoloration before bleaching.
- Bleaching was performed in three sessions of 15 minutes each per manufacturer's instructions.

## Outcomes

- **Enamel microhardness**: Highest initial values; decreased after bleaching; increased after pH cycle but not significantly _(not statistically significant; clinical: Bleaching reduces enamel microhardness; remineralization may partially recover it)_
- **Surface roughness**: Decreased after discoloration (P>0.05); increased significantly after pH cycle with significant differences between 4th measurement and other measurements (P<0.05) _(statistically significant; clinical: pH cycling with remineralizing toothpaste alters surface roughness of bleached enamel)_
