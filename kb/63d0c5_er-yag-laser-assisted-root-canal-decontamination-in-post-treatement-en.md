## Key Points

- Er:YAG laser was used to activate 5.25% sodium hypochlorite irrigation during endodontic retreatment of post-treatment chronic apical periodontitis at tooth 47.
- The laser-activated irrigation aimed to enhance root canal decontamination by exploiting photothermal effects on resistant microbial biofilms.
- Cone beam computed tomography (CBCT) was used to assess treatment efficacy by comparing pre- and postoperative radiographic images.
- At 3-month follow-up, radiographic assessment showed a tendency toward apical lesion regression and clinical improvement.
- No postoperative adverse events were observed during the follow-up period.
- Bacterial biofilm persistence in the endodontic system is identified as a key factor in post-treatment apical periodontitis.
