## Key Points

- Umbrella review of 9 systematic reviews evaluating laser-activated irrigation (LAI) in endodontics, covering Er:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG, Nd:YAG, and diode laser systems.
- Available evidence on laser-activated irrigation remains limited and heterogeneous; findings are inconsistent across included systematic reviews.
- Some systematic reviews suggest potential benefits in canal disinfection and postoperative outcomes, but results are not consistent.
- Major sources of heterogeneity include variability in laser wavelengths, study designs, and outcome measures.
- Laser-assisted irrigation should currently be regarded as an adjunct with unproven clinical benefit, pending robust evidence from well-designed studies with standardised protocols.
- Further standardised, high-quality clinical research is required before definitive conclusions on clinical effectiveness can be established.
- Methodological quality of included systematic reviews was assessed using the AMSTAR 2 tool.
- Review registered at PROSPERO (CRD42025599352); searched PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane, SciELO, Web of Science, and LILACS through December 2024.
