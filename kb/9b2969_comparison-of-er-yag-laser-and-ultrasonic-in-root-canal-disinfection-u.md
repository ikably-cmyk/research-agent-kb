## Key Points

- In vitro study comparing conventional irrigation (CI), passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI), and Er:YAG laser activated irrigation (LAI) in root canals accessed via conventional invasive access (CIA) or computer-guided minimally invasive access (MIA) in 66 extracted maxillary first molars.
- E. faecalis infection model was established after instrumentation to ProTaper Universal F2 size.
- Both PUI and LAI groups showed significantly fewer bacterial CFU counts than conventional irrigation alone (P < 0.05); no significant difference was found between PUI and LAI in root canal lumen disinfection.
- For dentin debris bacterial counts after enlargement to F3, the Er:YAG LAI group had the least CFU counts, with a statistically significant antagonism (F = 7.224, P = 0.002).
- Minimally invasive access with Er:YAG laser or ultrasonic achieved disinfection comparable to conventional access with the same adjunct.
- Conventional irrigation through minimally invasive access (group 2A) was significantly better than through conventional access (group 1A) (P < 0.05).
- Er:YAG laser activated irrigation showed superior removal of bacteria from dentinal tubules compared to ultrasonic, and was described as easier to operate.

## Outcomes

- **Bacterial CFU counts in root canal lumen after disinfection**: PUI and LAI groups had significantly fewer CFU than CI group (P < 0.05); no significant difference between PUI and LAI (P > 0.05) _(statistically significant; clinical: Both ultrasonic and Er:YAG laser irrigation improve root canal disinfection over conventional irrigation alone)_
- **Bacterial CFU counts in dentin debris after enlargement to F3**: Er:YAG LAI group had the least CFU counts; significant antagonism F = 7.224, P = 0.002 _(statistically significant; clinical: Er:YAG laser may be superior to ultrasonic for removing bacteria embedded in dentinal tubules)_
- **Comparison of disinfection between CIA and MIA subgroups**: No significant difference between CIA and MIA for PUI or LAI subgroups; MIA-CI was significantly better than CIA-CI (P < 0.05) _(statistically significant; clinical: Er:YAG laser and ultrasonic irrigation compensate for access cavity design limitations in minimally invasive approaches)_
