**Resultado principal:** Determinar la densidad de energía mínima (umbral de ablación) necesaria para producir ablación en esmalte dental humano con Er:YAG (2940 nm) y Er:YSGG (2790 nm), como base para aplicaciones subablativas (prevención de caries, aumento de resistencia al ácido) y ablativas (preparación cavitaria).

- **Ablation threshold of Er:YAG (2940 nm) in dental enamel**: 9–11 J/cm² (range); 10 J/cm² at 80% sensitivity by logistic regression _(statistically significant; clinical: Defines the minimum energy density required for enamel ablation; energies below this can be used for sub-ablative applications such as caries prevention.)_
- **Ablation threshold of Er:YSGG (2790 nm) in dental enamel**: 10–14 J/cm² (range); 13 J/cm² at 80% sensitivity by logistic regression _(statistically significant; clinical: Slightly higher threshold than Er:YAG; broader transition range indicates less predictable ablation onset.)_
- **Regional variation in ablation threshold across dental crown**: Cervical enamel ablated at lower energy densities than central buccal/occlusal enamel; effect more pronounced with Er:YSGG _(clinical: Clinicians must account for regional differences in enamel mineralization when setting laser parameters.)_
- **Microcrack formation with Er:YSGG below ablation threshold**: Microcracks observed in prism-free outer enamel layer at sub-ablative energy densities with Er:YSGG _(clinical: Potential structural risk with Er:YSGG at sub-ablative energies; clinical implications require further study.)_

**Nivel de evidencia:** Bajo — estudio in vitro con 130 muestras de esmalte ex vivo; sin grupo control clínico; sistema láser experimental no comercial; resultados no directamente transferibles a práctica clínica.
