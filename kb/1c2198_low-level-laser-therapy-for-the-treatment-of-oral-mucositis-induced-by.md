## Key Points

- Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) significantly reduces the severity of oral mucositis in patients undergoing hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT).
- Meta-analysis of 3 RCTs (from 230 initially identified papers) showed a standardized mean difference of -1.34 (95% CI: -1.98 to -0.98) in mucositis severity favoring laser therapy over placebo.
- Oral mucositis is a common and debilitating complication of HSCT, involving inflammation and ulceration of the oral mucosa, causing pain, dysphagia, and increased infection risk.
- LLLT application resulted in decreased severity of oral mucositis induced by radiation and chemotherapy in transplant patients.
- The review included only studies with a placebo-treated control group, improving internal validity, though only 3 studies met inclusion criteria, limiting generalizability.
- The authors conclude that LLLT has excellent efficacy in relieving symptoms and severity of post-transplant mucositis.
