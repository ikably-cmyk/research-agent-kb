## Key Points

- Retrospective review of 36 rosacea patients receiving concurrent laser/IPL therapy and systemic doxycycline (standard or anti-inflammatory dose).
- No adverse reactions related to photosensitivity or phototoxicity were observed when combining doxycycline with pulsed dye laser (PDL) or intense pulsed light (IPL).
- All patients achieved some degree of clinical clearance of rosacea signs and symptoms.
- The theoretical risk of photosensitivity from doxycycline combined with laser/light therapy was not realized in this cohort.
- Combination of doxycycline with laser or nonlaser light therapy is presented as a valid treatment approach for rosacea.
