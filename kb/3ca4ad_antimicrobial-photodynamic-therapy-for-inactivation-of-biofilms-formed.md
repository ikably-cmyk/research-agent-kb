- **aPDT with TBO (100 mg/l) + HeNe/LED on S. mutans biofilms (3–10 days)**: 2–5 log10 CFU reduction depending on biofilm age, light source, and irradiation period _(clinical: Achieved ≥3 log10 (ASM threshold) in some conditions)_
- **aPDT with MB (25 μg/ml) + diode laser (1W, 665 nm) on polymicrobial root canal biofilm**: 80% CFU reduction (Fimple et al., 2008) _(clinical: Did not reach 3 log10 threshold; authors suggest parameter optimization needed)_
- **aPDT with TBO (25 μg/ml) + diode laser (100 mW, 625 nm) on E. faecalis root canal biofilms (4–8 weeks)**: Total elimination below CFU detection limit _(clinical: Complete eradication achieved; clinically significant)_
- **Er:YAG (2940 nm, 100 mJ, 15 Hz, 40 s) vs. aPDT on E. faecalis biofilms**: Er:YAG 100 mJ: 4 log10; Er:YAG 50 mJ and Nd:YAG: <1 log10; NaOCl 2.5%: >6 log10 _(clinical: NaOCl superior to both laser and aPDT; Er:YAG dose-dependent effect)_
- **SAPYR (100 μM) + dental LED (460±20 nm, 600 mW/cm², 120 s) on E. faecalis biofilms (72 h)**: ≥5 log10 CFU reduction in both monospecies and polyspecies biofilms _(clinical: Exceeds ASM threshold; promising novel PS)_
- **CSRB (0.3 mg/ml) + 540 nm fiber (40 J/cm²) on E. faecalis and P. aeruginosa biofilms (7 days)**: E. faecalis: ~5 log10; P. aeruginosa: ~9 log10 _(clinical: Substantially higher efficacy than unconjugated RB or MB)_
