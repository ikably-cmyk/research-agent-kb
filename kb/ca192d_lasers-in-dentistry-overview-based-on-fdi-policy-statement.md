## Key Points

- Erbium lasers (Er:YAG, Er,Cr:YSGG) demonstrate effective and selective caries ablation, smear layer removal, and improved adhesion in restorative dentistry.
- Laser-activated irrigation protocols such as photon-induced photoacoustic streaming (PIPS) and shock wave-enhanced emission photoacoustic streaming (SWEEPS) enhance irrigant penetration and biofilm disruption in endodontics.
- In prosthodontics and orthodontics, erbium lasers allow minimally invasive debonding of crowns, veneers, and brackets while preserving underlying structures.
- In periodontology and peri-implantitis, lasers improve debridement, support regeneration, and enhance wound healing.
- Photobiomodulation (PBM) contributes to pain reduction, inflammation control, and tissue repair.
- Outcomes vary with wavelength, energy settings, and clinical protocols, underscoring the need for standardization.
- The review is aligned with the FDI Policy Statement on dental lasers to support globally consistent clinical practice.
- Further high-quality randomized controlled trials are necessary to define standardized laser protocols across dental specialties.
