## Key Points

- The study evaluated microtensile bond strength (µTBS) of three universal adhesives (Futurabond U, AdheSE Universal, Xeno Select) applied in etch-and-rinse mode on sound and irradiated human dentin.
- Dentin specimens from 135 extracted third molars were divided into control (no irradiation), low-dose (5 Gy), and high-dose (60 Gy) irradiation groups.
- ANOVA showed significant effects of both dentin irradiation and adhesive type on µTBS (P<0.01), but Tukey's post-hoc test found no statistically significant differences between irradiation groups for any of the three adhesives (P>0.05).
- A trend toward decreased µTBS was observed in highly irradiated dentin across nearly all groups, but this did not reach statistical significance.
- The authors conclude that etch-and-rinse application mode of universal adhesives may be a viable option for patients who have undergone radiation therapy for head and neck tumors.

## Outcomes

- **Microtensile bond strength (µTBS) of universal adhesives on irradiated vs. sound dentin**: No statistically significant difference in µTBS between irradiation groups (control, 5 Gy, 60 Gy) for any of the three universal adhesives in etch-and-rinse mode (P>0.05, Tukey's test), despite a general trend toward decreased µTBS in highly irradiated dentin. _(not statistically significant; clinical: Etch-and-rinse mode of universal adhesives appears unaffected by tumor irradiation doses, suggesting clinical viability in post-radiation therapy patients.)_
